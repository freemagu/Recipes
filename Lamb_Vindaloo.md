# Lamb Vindaloo (Goan Style)

> Goan-style lamb vindaloo with sous-vide shoulder - no tomato, coconut, or cream.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 kg lamb shoulder (rolled, tied)
- [ ] Fine salt (about 16 g for sous vide)
- [ ] 8 dried Kashmiri chillies
- [ ] 2–3 dried bird's eye chillies (optional, for heat)
- [ ] 6 garlic cloves
- [ ] 30 g fresh ginger
- [ ] 2 tsp cumin seeds
- [ ] 1 tsp coriander seeds
- [ ] 1 tsp brown mustard seeds
- [ ] 6 black peppercorns
- [ ] 4 cloves
- [ ] 1 small cinnamon stick (2–3 cm)
- [ ] 3 tbsp white wine vinegar
- [ ] 2 large onions, finely chopped
- [ ] 3 tbsp ghee or beef dripping
- [ ] 1 tsp turmeric
- [ ] Water or lamb stock (small amount)

## Method

- [ ] 1 — Salt the lamb (about 16 g total), leave string on, vacuum seal.
- [ ] 2 — Sous vide at 72°C for 13–14 hours.
- [ ] 3 — Remove lamb from bag, reserve juices, cut off string, cut into 4–5 cm chunks, pat dry.
- [ ] 4 — Sear lamb hard in ghee or dripping until well browned. Set aside.
- [ ] 5 — Soak dried chillies in boiling water for 20 minutes.
- [ ] 6 — Grind chillies, garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander, mustard seeds, peppercorns, cloves, cinnamon, vinegar, and a little soaking water into a thick paste.
- [ ] 7 — Fry onions slowly in ghee or dripping until deep golden brown.
- [ ] 8 — Add turmeric, stir 30 seconds, then add vindaloo paste.
- [ ] 9 — Cook paste until darkened and oil separates.
- [ ] 10 — Add lamb and reserved juices; add just enough water or stock to coat.
- [ ] 11 — Simmer gently, uncovered, for 30–45 minutes until thick and glossy.
- [ ] 12 — Taste and adjust salt. If acidity is harsh, add up to 1 tsp sugar only if needed.

## Notes

- No tomatoes, no coconut milk, no cream.
- Better the next day.
