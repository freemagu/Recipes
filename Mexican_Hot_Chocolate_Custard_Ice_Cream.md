# Mexican Hot Chocolate Custard Ice Cream

> Spiced Mexican hot-chocolate custard ice cream with cinnamon and a hint of chili.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 500 ml Vollrahm (35% fat) (2 cups heavy cream), divided
- [ ] 30 g unsweetened cocoa powder (¼ cup)
- [ ] 170 g dark chocolate (~60% cocoa)
- [ ] 375 ml whole milk (1½ cups)
- [ ] 150 g sugar (¾ cup)
- [ ] ⅛ tsp sea salt
- [ ] 3 large egg yolks
- [ ] 1 medium cinnamon stick (~8–10 cm)
- [ ] ½ vanilla pod (scrape seeds + steep pod), or 1 tsp extract
- [ ] Pinch of cayenne or chili powder (optional)

## Method

- [ ] 1 — In a saucepan, whisk 250 ml cream (1 cup) with cocoa powder. Bring to a boil, whisk 30 sec, then remove from heat. Add chopped chocolate, whisk smooth. Add remaining 250 ml cream (1 cup). Pour into a mixing bowl with a strainer on top.
- [ ] 2 — In another saucepan, combine milk, sugar, and salt. Add cinnamon stick + vanilla pod (with seeds). Heat until steaming, remove from heat, cover, steep 15 minutes. Remove stick and pod.
- [ ] 3 — Whisk yolks in a bowl. Slowly whisk in 120 ml (½ cup) warm spiced milk. Return yolk mix to saucepan, whisking constantly. Cook over medium heat, stirring, until thickened (170°F / coats spatula).
- [ ] 4 — Pour custard through strainer into chocolate mixture. Stir well. Chill 6 hours or overnight.
- [ ] 5 — Churn in ice cream maker 30–40 minutes until thick and frozen.
- [ ] 6 — Transfer to airtight container, freeze at least 6 hours. Serve dusted with cinnamon and cocadas.

## Notes

- Makes ~1.5 litres / ~12 servings.
- Serve dusted with cinnamon, alongside cocadas.
- Optional garnishes: Tajín chili-lime seasoning, dark chocolate shavings.
