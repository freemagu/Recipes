# Vanilla Panna Cotta with Light Pomegranate Glaze

> Vanilla panna cotta with a light pomegranate glaze.

## Ingredients

### Panna Cotta

- [ ] 375 ml heavy cream (1.5 cups)
- [ ] 125 ml whole milk (0.5 cups)
- [ ] 40 g white sugar (about 3 tbsp)
- [ ] 2 tsp gelatin powder (about 7 g)
- [ ] 3 tbsp cold water (for blooming gelatin)
- [ ] 1 vanilla bean (split) or 1.5 tsp good vanilla extract

### Pomegranate Glaze

- [ ] 250 ml pomegranate juice (1 cup)
- [ ] 2 tbsp white sugar

### To Serve

- [ ] Pomegranate seeds
- [ ] Dark chocolate, frozen (optional, very small amount)

## Method

### Panna Cotta

- [ ] 1 — Sprinkle gelatin powder over 3 tbsp cold water. Leave 5–10 minutes to bloom.
- [ ] 2 — In a saucepan, combine cream, milk, sugar, and vanilla.
- [ ] 3 — Heat gently until hot and steaming. Do not boil.
- [ ] 4 — Remove from heat.
- [ ] 5 — Stir in bloomed gelatin until fully dissolved and smooth.
- [ ] 6 — Pour into 3 small glasses or ramekins.
- [ ] 7 — Refrigerate at least 4 hours (or overnight) until softly set.

### Pomegranate Glaze

- [ ] 1 — Add pomegranate juice and sugar to a small pan.
- [ ] 2 — Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
- [ ] 3 — Reduce until lightly syrupy (coats the back of a spoon).
- [ ] 4 — Cool completely.

### To Finish

- [ ] 1 — Spoon a thin layer of cooled glaze over each panna cotta.
- [ ] 2 — Garnish with a few pomegranate seeds.
- [ ] 3 — Optional: shave a tiny amount of dark chocolate on top just before serving.

## Notes

- Keep portions small. This dessert should feel light.
- Skip rum or alcohol for a cleaner finish.
- Best served straight from the fridge, not ice-cold.
- Serves 3 adults.
