# Banana–Coconut Protein Custards

> Sous-vide banana-coconut protein custards, set in jars for grab-and-go.

## Nutrition

| Macro    | Whole recipe | Per serving (÷4) |
| -------- | ------------ | ---------------- |
| Calories | 1424         | 356              |
| Protein  | 94 g         | 24 g             |
| Fat      | 102 g        | 26 g             |
| Carbs    | 36 g         | 9 g              |
| Sugar    | 23 g         | 6 g              |

## Ingredients

- [ ] 8 large eggs
- [ ] 1 medium banana (~120 g, peeled)
- [ ] 300 ml full-fat coconut milk
- [ ] 30 g collagen peptides (hydrolysed)
- [ ] 7 g gelatin powder (unhydrolysed)
- [ ] 35 g cold water (for blooming)
- [ ] 8–15 g monk-fruit/erythritol blend, to taste
- [ ] ¼ tsp fine salt
- [ ] Vanilla or cinnamon (optional)

## Method

- [ ] 1 — Preheat water bath to 80°C.
- [ ] 2 — Bloom gelatin: sprinkle 7 g gelatin over 35 g cold water; rest 5–10 min.
- [ ] 3 — Warm & dissolve: heat coconut milk to 50–60°C (no boil). Whisk in collagen until smooth, then stir in bloomed gelatin until clear. Remove from heat.
- [ ] 4 — Blend base: blend eggs, banana, salt, sweetener (and vanilla/cinnamon if using) just until smooth; avoid foam.
- [ ] 5 — Combine & strain: whisk warm coconut mix into egg blend; strain through a fine sieve into a jug.
- [ ] 6 — Jar up: fill jars to ~210–220 ml (leave 1–2 cm headspace). Close fingertip-tight.
- [ ] 7 — Cook: submerge and cook 60 min at 80°C (centre should wobble slightly when tapped).
- [ ] 8 — Chill to set: rest 10 min, then ice-bath 20–30 min. Refrigerate 2–3 h before serving.
- [ ] 9 — Store refrigerated; best within 3–4 days.

## Notes

- Yield: 4 × 250 ml jars (fill each to ~210–220 ml).
- Cook temp/time: 80°C · ~60 min (softer 50 min · firmer 70 min).
- Per-jar macros (estimate): ~356 kcal, ~23.4 g protein, ~25.5 g fat, ~9.0 g carbs, ~5.8 g sugar, ~0.8 g fibre.
- Softer set: cook 50 min or reduce gelatin to 5 g.
- Firmer set: cook 70 min or increase gelatin to 8–10 g.
- Increase protein: raise collagen to 40 g and add +40–60 ml coconut milk; cook to same gentle wobble.
