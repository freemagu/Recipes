# Light Shortbread Biscuits

> Light, crisp shortbread biscuits to serve alongside panna cotta.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 120 g unsalted butter, softened (not melted)
- [ ] 50 g white sugar
- [ ] 140 g plain flour
- [ ] 40 g rice flour or cornflour
- [ ] Small pinch fine sea salt
- [ ] 0.5 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

## Method

### Dough

- [ ] 1 — Preheat oven to 160°C (fan) or 170°C conventional.
- [ ] 2 — Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
- [ ] 3 — Cream butter and sugar together until smooth and just lightened. Do not whip.
- [ ] 4 — Mix in salt and vanilla (if using).
- [ ] 5 — Add plain flour and rice flour.
- [ ] 6 — Mix gently until the dough just comes together. Stop immediately.

### Shaping

- [ ] 1 — Roll dough into a log about 4 cm in diameter.
- [ ] 2 — Wrap in parchment or cling film.
- [ ] 3 — Chill in fridge for 20–30 minutes to firm up.
- [ ] 4 — Slice into thin rounds, about 5–6 mm thick.
- [ ] 5 — Place on tray with a little space between each.

### Baking

- [ ] 1 — Bake for 18–22 minutes.
- [ ] 2 — Biscuits should stay pale with just the faintest golden edge.
- [ ] 3 — Remove and cool fully on the tray.

## Notes

- Designed to sit next to panna cotta.
- Serve one biscuit per person only. Plate beside the panna cotta glass or lean lightly on the plate, not inside.
- No chocolate dip, no sugar dusting, no extras.
- Ideal with coffee or green tea.
- Reduced butter compared to classic shortbread; rice flour gives a light, crisp snap.
